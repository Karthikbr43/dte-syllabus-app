UNIT I: Introduction
What is an Algorithm?, Fundamentals of Algorithmic problem solving, Important problem types, Fundamental data structures.
<br><b>[08 Hrs] [20 Marks] </b>

UNIT II: Fundamentals of the Analysis of Algorithm Efficiency
Analysis Framework, Measuring the input size, Units for measuring Running time, Orders of Growth, Worst-case, Best-case and Average-case efficiencies, Asymptotic Notations and Basic Efficiency classes, Informal Introduction, O-notation, Ω-notation, θ-notation, mathematical analysis of non-recursive algorithms, mathematical analysis of recursive algorithms.
<br><b>[10 Hrs] [30 Marks] </b>
 
UNIT III: Brute Force
Selection Sort and Bubble Sort, Sequential search and Brute-Force String matching, Closest pair and Convex-Hull problems by Brute Force, Exhaustive Search.
<br><b>[06 Hrs] [15 Marks] </b>

UNIT IV: Divide-and-Conquer
Merge Sort, Quick Sort, Binary Search, Binary Tree traversals and related properties.
<br><b>[08 Hrs] [20 Marks] </b>

UNIT V: Decrease-and-Conquer and Greedy Technique
<b>Decrease-and-Conquer</b>: Insertion Sort, Depth-First Search and Breadth-First Search, Topological Sorting.
<br><b>Greedy Technique</b>: Prim’s Algorithm, Kruskal’s Algorithm, Dijkstra’s Algorithm, Huffman Trees.
<br><b>[10 Hrs] [30 Marks] </b>

UNIT VI: Limitations of Algorithm Power and Coping with its Limitations
<b>Limitations of Algorithm Power</b>: Lower-Bound arguments, Decision trees, P,NP and NP complete problems, Challenges of numerical Algorithms.
<br><b>Coping with limitations of Algorithm Power</b>: Backtracking, Branch-and-Bound, Approximation Algorithms for NP-Hard problems.
<br><b>[10 Hrs] [30 Marks] </b>
