UNIT I: Introduction to geology and Study of earth
Definition, Identify different branches of geology, Understand the importance and scope of geology, describe our solar system, describe the formation of earth, and identify its parts, measure the age of earth by radiometric dating and understand the type of atmosphere.
<br><b>[05 Hrs] [10 Marks] </b>

UNIT II: Mineralogy
Define mineralogy, describe physical properties of minerals such as habit, color, streak, crystal system, luster, cleavage, fracture, hardness, specific gravity, magnetic property, identify the physical properties of minerals such as quartz, feldspar, mica, asbestos, agate, dolomite, fluorite, gypsum, galena, graphite, hematite, magnetite, pyrite, talc, zircon, bauxite, corundum, chromites.
<br><b>[10 Hrs] [30 Marks] </b>

UNIT III: Petrology
Definition of rocks, its classification based on their origin, describe the formation, texture and structure of igneous, sedimentary and metamorphic rock, and name the forms of igneous (granite, diorite, basalt, hornblende, obsidian, pegmatite, syenite, Gabor, dolerite, peridotite), sedimentary (conglomerate, dolomite, limestone, sandstone, shale, laterite, rock salt, chert, breccias) and metamorphic rocks (marble, slate, soapstone, quartzite, gneiss, phyllite, schis, amphibolite)  with their uses.
<br><b>[10 Hrs] [30 Marks] </b>

UNIT IV: Structural geology
Introduction, dip, apparent dip and true dip, strike, definition, parts and types of folds, faults and joints.
<br><b>[10 Hrs] [30 Marks] </b>

UNIT V: Physical geology
Introduction, weathering – physical weathering (Frost, heating and cooling and organisms) and chemical weathering. Erosion - its type causes and effects. Glaciers – types, its effects. Earth quake and its causes and affects
<br><b>[07 Hrs] [20 Marks] </b>

UNIT VI: Crystallography
Definition of crystal, elements of crystal, crystallographic axes, axial and symmetry character, interfacial angle, crystal system.
<br><b>[10 Hrs] [25 Marks] </b>
