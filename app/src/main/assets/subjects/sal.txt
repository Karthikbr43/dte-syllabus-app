UNIT I: SURVEYING	
Introduction, Definition, objectives, classification, principles of surveying; Conventional signs; Surveying instruments, their care and adjustments
<br><b>[03 Hrs] [10 Marks] </b>

UNIT II: CHAINSURVEYING	
Principles, operations in chain surveying Types of chains and tapes1) ranging – direct and indirect method,2) chaining – on flat and sloping method ,3) offsets- perpendicular and oblique
<br><b>[09 Hrs] [25 Marks] </b>

UNIT III: COMPASS SURVEYING	
Definitions, concepts, bearings-systems of bearings and their conversion, fore bearing, back bearing; prismatic compass-temporary adjustments, measurement of included angle Local attraction and its elimination; Closing error, meridian and its types
<br><b>[15 Hrs] [40 Marks] </b>

UNIT IV: LEVELLING and CONTOURING	
Definitions; Levelling instruments, different levels and staves; 1) dumpy level fig and 2) staff metric fig; temporary adjustments of dumpy level; calculation of RL-using HI methodand Rise andfall method of levelling; Contouring Definitions; Contour interval and horizontal equivalent; Characteristics of contour lines; Direct and indirect methods of contouring; Use of contour maps
<br><b>[15 Hrs] [40 Marks] </b>

UNIT V: THE ODOLITE SURVEYING	
Definitions terms used measurement of Measurement of horizontal angles. Vertical angles, bearings
<br><b>[05 Hrs] [15 Marks] </b>

UNIT VI: MODERN SURVEYING
GPS, Total station, uses of minor instruments
<br><b>[05 Hrs] [15 Marks] </b>
