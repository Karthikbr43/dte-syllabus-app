UNIT I: Introduction to Ceramic Products and Processing
Definition, Classification, properties and application of following traditional and advanced ceramic products with examples. 
<br>a. Traditional Ceramics – White wares (terracotta, earthenware, stoneware and porcelains), cements, glass, refractories, abrasives. 
<br>b. Advanced Ceramic products – Electrical and Electronic, Magnetic, Mechanical, Special Refractories, Optical, Chemical, Bio-Ceramics and Nuclear Ceramic Products. 
<br><b>[06 Hrs] [15 Marks] </b>

UNIT II: Additives
Define and briefly explain the role with examples - Surfactants, Water, Organic Liquids, Flocculants and Deflocculants, Binders, and Plasticizers, foaming and antifoaming agents, lubricants and preservatives. Study the thixotropic characters.
<br><b>[08 Hrs] [20 Marks] </b>

UNIT III: Batch Preparation
Definition of batch preparation, Dry mix and Wet mix. Demonstrate methods of body preparation by dry and wet method, State and list the uses of Brongniart’s Formulae, Describe Defects and Remedy in Dry mix. Enumerate the advantages and disadvantages of dry and wet method. Explain Weathering, Ageing and Souring of body’s, Tempering and Pugging (ordinary and deairing). 
<br><b>[08 Hrs] [25 Marks] </b>

UNIT IV: Forming
Illustrate the shaping process with sketches and constructional features of machinery for following process: 
<br>a. Plastic Shaping –Hand moulding with potter’s wheel, Jiggering and Jolleying, 
<br>b. Semi-plastic Shaping - pressing, extrusion, injection moulding and turning. 
<br>c. Dry Pressing - study the Particle packing characteristics, outline the important parameters of pressing (powder, die and pressure characteristics), explain stages in pressing. Types of presses: Toggle press, Fly press/screw press, Friction press, Hydraulic press, Isostatic press and Hot isostatic press. 
<br>d. Casting - Slip casting (solid, hollow and Core) and Tape casting. Explain Surface finishing methods - Trimming, Smoothening, Grinding, Blanking, Punching and Laminating. 
<br><b>[15 Hrs] [45 Marks] </b>

UNIT V: Drying and Firing
Definition, mechanism of drying, classification of driers based on heat source, type of material (raw material / Product), list various driers, mode of operation. Describe Firing, biscuit firing, glost firing, sintering, vitrification and stages of firing. List various furnaces and kilns with their field of application. Discuss the general constructional features of drier and furnace.
<br><b>[09 Hrs] [25 Marks] </b>

UNIT IV: Process flowcharts
Illustrate and explain general Manufacturing Process (dry and wet) of Ceramic Products with flow chart. 
<br>Describe the following process with flow diagram - Bone china plates by jiggering, Ceramic cups by jolleying, Terracotta roofing tile by pressing, Insulators by turning, Wash basins by slip casting, Refractories by semi-dry pressing, Floor tiles by dry pressing, Substrates by tape casting, Spark plugs by isostatic pressing.
<br><b>[06 Hrs] [15 Marks] </b>
