UNIT I: STONES
Classification of rocks, Quarrying of stones by wedging and blasting, Tests on stone (Acid test, Attrition, crushing, impact and water absorption), Characteristics of a good building stone, Deterioration and Preservation of stones
<br><b>[10 Hrs] [28 Marks] </b>

UNIT II: BRICKS
Composition of good brick earth and harmful ingredients, Manufacture of bricks, Burning of bricks by clamps-intermittent (down draught) and continuous kiln (Hoffman’s), Classification of bricks as per I S, Test on bricks (Field tests, Crushing strength, absorption, shape and size, efflorescence test), Requirements of good bricks, Substitutes for bricks – Cement concrete blocks (solid), Production process of solid blocks. Fire clay/Refractory bricks, Calcium Silicate Bricks (properties and uses).
<br><b>[11 Hrs] [31 Marks] </b>

UNIT III: TIMBER
 Classification of timber based on mode of growth, Cross- section of an exogenous tree, Properties of good timber, Defects in timber, Preservation of Timber, Seasoning of Timber, Conversion of timber and Market forms(types and uses).
<br><b>[10 Hrs] [28 Marks] </b>

UNIT IV: CEMENT 
Composition of ordinary Portland cement, Functions of ingredient of cement, Manufacture of OPC (Mixing of raw materials by dry process, Burning andGrinding), Storage of cement, Field tests on cement, Types of cement and its uses. 
<br><b>[08 Hrs] [22 Marks] </b>

UNIT V: PAINTS, VARNISH AND DISTEMPER
Objects, characteristics andIngredients of paints, varnishes and distemper 
<br><b>[04 Hrs] [11 Marks] </b>

UNIT VI: FERROUS, NONFERROUS AND ALLOY
FERROUS METALS: Properties and uses of Cast iron, Wrought iron,  Mild steel Tor steel, TMT, High tensile steel, Market forms of structural steel NONFERROUS METALS:- Properties and uses of Copper, Aluminum, Zinc and Tin ALLOY: - Types, properties and uses - aluminum alloy, copper alloy and steel alloy
<br><b>[09 Hrs] [25 Marks] </b>
