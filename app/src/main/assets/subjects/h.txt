UNIT I: FLUIDS, PRESSURE AND ITS MEASUREMENTS AND HYDROSTATICS 
Fluid-definition, types and properties (simple problems).
<br>Pressure and its measurements-Definition of fluid pressure, intensity of pressure and pressure head, Pascal’s law, atmospheric pressure, gauge pressure, absolute pressure, vacuum pressure and their relations, Measurement of pressure: Piezometer, Manometer, differential manometer, (simple problems).
<br>Hydrostatics-Total pressure and centre of pressure on vertical planes like, triangular , rectangular and circular plane surfaces (simple problems).
<br><b>[10 Hrs] [30 Marks] </b>

UNIT II: FLOW OF FLUIDS
Definition of discharge, Types of flow and their comparison, uniform and non-uniform flow, steady and unsteady flow, stream line and turbulent flow, rotational and irrotational flow, compressible and incompressible flow. 
<br>Continuity equation- simple problems.
<br>Energies in fluid motion and their heads- Potential, Kinetic and Pressure head
<br>Bernoulli’s theorem (without proof), assumptions and its limitations–simple problems. Horizontal venturimeter, theory and simple problems.
<br><b>[10 Hrs] [30 Marks] </b>

UNIT III: FLOW THROUGH ORIFICES, NOTCHES AND WEIRS.
Definition, types of orifices, vena contracta, hydraulic co-efficient and their relationship (simple problems), Flow through only circular orifice (simple problems without derivation), Definition and types of mouthpiece (only theory).
<br>Definition of notch, and its types, discharge calculation for rectangular, triangular and trapezoidal notch (simple problems without derivation).
<br>Definition of weir, types, discharge over rectangular and cippolitte weir (simple problems without derivation)
<br><b>[13 Hrs] [35 Marks] </b>

UNIT IV: FLOW THROUGH PIPES
Loss of fluid friction, Types of losses-major and minor losses, Darcy’s and Chezy’s formula for head loss due to friction (simple problems). 
<br>Hydraulic gradient and Total Energy Line.
<br>Flow through single pipe, Flow from one reservoir to another reservoir through single pipe, (simple problems).
<br>Water hammer, surge tanks (only theory).
<br><b>[07 Hrs] [20 Marks] </b>

UNIT V: FLOW THROUGH CHANNELS
Definition and classification of channels.
<br>Discharge through channels using Chezy’s and Manning’s formula (no derivation – simple problems only). 
<br>Most economical sections for rectangular and trapezoidal channels (simple problems).
<br><b>[08 Hrs] [20 Marks] </b>

UNIT VI: PUMPS AND TURBINES
Working principle of Reciprocating and centrifugal pumps ( Only Theory)
<br>Turbine, types and its working principle of Pelton and Francis turbine ( Only Theory)
<br><b>[04 Hrs] [10 Marks] </b>
