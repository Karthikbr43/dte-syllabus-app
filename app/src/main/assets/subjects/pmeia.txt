UNIT I: CONSTRUCTION PLANNING & ORGANIZATION     
	Construction Team: Owner, Engineer & Contractor- Their Relationship.
<br>	Construction stages, Schedules & Job layouts, Pre tender & Post tender planning.
<br>	Definition & Need of Organisation, Types & Organisation chart in PWD, Construction Corporations & Private Construction Companies.
<br>	Administrative approval and its Requirements.
<br>	Definition of site Order book, Hindrance Register, Drawing Register.
<br><br><b>[08 Hrs] [20 Marks] </b>

UNIT II: CONTRACTS & TENDERS 
	Contracts- Types of Contracts & departmental Execution of works. 
<br>	General conditions of contract, contract agreement, Conditions for failure of contract and its extension, Termination of contract.
<br>	Concept of RA bill- submission, scrutiny and payment.
<br>	Tender- Procedure for inviting tender, Tender Notice & Tender documents, EMD, SMD & Guaranties, Scrutiny of Tenders & e-Tendering.
<br>	Mobilization advance, conditions for Escalation of materials.
<br><br><b>[10 Hrs] [30 Marks] </b>

UNIT III: PLANNING, SCHEDULING & NETWORK ANALYSIS:
	Fundamentals of CPM and PERT, differences and elements of network analysis.
<br>	Scheduling – Definition of Scheduling Resource Planning (MMMT- Man-Material-Money-Time).
<br><br><b>[06 Hrs] [20 Marks] </b>

UNIT IV: CONVENTIONS OF CONSTRUCTION INDUSTRY 
	SAFETY-Importance of safety, Causes & effects of accidents in construction Industry, Safety procedures and check list, Safety meetings, Safety measures to be adopted while execution and safety legal implications.
<br>	STORES-Classification of Stores. Issues, Indents & Bin cards,
<br>	MEASUREMENTS-Measurement Book, Recording of measurements, Pre measurements & Check measurements
<br>	INSPECTION-Principals of inspection, Stages of inspection while execution.
<br>	QUALITY CONTROL- Functions of Quality Control in construction industry. Importance of Total Quality Management, ISO9000 certification procedure.
<br><br><b>[13 Hrs] [40 Marks] </b>

UNIT V: ENTERPRENEURSHIP
	Definition –Concept- Roles-Expectations of Entrepreneurship.
<br>	Motivational needs of the entrepreneur.
<br>	Characteristics of an entrepreneur.
<br><br><b>[05 Hrs] [10 Marks] </b>

UNIT VI: ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT ASSESSMENT 
	Environmental management
<br>	Terminology in E I A
<br>	Objectives of E I A
<br>	Process and methodologies of E I A
<br>	Environmental Impact Statement
<br>	Risk analysis

<br><br><b>Suggested Student Activity:</b>

<br><br>1. Visit any nearby construction site & interact with the construction team regarding type of structure & its organisation structure.
<br>2. Visit any Govt. Construction Department interact about the present tendering process and awarding of contract.
<br>3. Collection of tender notices published in newspapers for various items of civil engineering works( at least 5) write salient features of them.
<br>4. Study the application of Bar Chart, CPM & PERT technique Used in construction projects.
<br>5. Prepare a planning schedule for the nearby on going construction activity with the help of PRIMA VEERA.
<br>6. Visit any nearby PWD/PERD /KIARDL/KHB office or any construction company, collect the documents (BOQ, M B, Tender, SR, lead statement etc….) related to the project.
<br>7. Collect quality management standards pertaining to ISO 9001, ISO 14001 & OHSAS 18001 & prepare a report.
<br>8. Drafting a tender notices for construction of a civil engineering work
<br>9. Preparation of tender document for the building.(detailed estimate prepared for R.C.C. building in estimating and costing shall be used )
<br>10. Collection of various account forms from PWD & Prepare a report on it
<br>11. Prepare a report on store procedure and account producer of PWD.( For it a Guest lecture of PWD official may be arranged. )
<br>12. Prepare EIA for ongoing project.
