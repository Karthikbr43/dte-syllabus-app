UNIT I: Introduction to RCC, Analysis of singly reinforced, doubly reinforced beams, T-beam
<b>Introduction</b>: Concept of reinforced concrete structures, Different grades of concrete and steel used in RCC. Load and loading standards as per IS:875. Differentiate between ultimate load method, working stress method and limit state method of design.
<br><br><b>Design Based on Limit State Method</b>:Fundamentals of Limit State Method, types of limit state, Introduction to stress block parameters, Assumptions in the theory of simple bending for RCC beams, Neutral Axis, Moment of resistance, critical neutral axis, actual neutral axis, concept of balanced, under reinforced and over-reinforced sections.
Partial safety factors, characteristic strength of materials and loads, Flexural strength, Shear Strength, Development Length of bars, Deflection and cracking, Design requirements, Side face reinforcement, Nominal Cover to reinforcement.
<br><br><b>Analysis of Beams</b>: Analysis of the following beam as per IS:456-2000(Simply supported and cantilever beams)
<br>(i) Singly reinforced Beams
<br>(ii) Doubly reinforced Beams and its necessity.
<br>(iii) T-beams: Structural behaviour of a beam and slab floor laid monolithically, Rules for the design of T-Beams, Economical depth of T-Beams, Strength of T-Beams, concept of L-beam.
<br><br><b>[12 Hrs] [45 Marks] </b>

UNIT II: Design of  singly reinforced, doubly reinforced beams, Lintels
<b>Design of singly reinforced concrete beams</b> as per IS: 456 from the given data such as span, load and properties of materials used. (Design for shear  Two legged vertical stirrups only and check for deflection)
<br><br><b>Design of doubly reinforced concrete beams</b> as per IS: 456 from the given data such as span, load and properties of materials used. (Design only for shear)
<br><br><b>Problems</b> on simply supported and cantilever beams.
<br><br><b>[08 Hrs] [15 Marks] </b>

UNIT III: Design of RCC Slabs:
Structural behaviour of one way and two way slabs under uniformly distributed load (UDL), Types of end supports, Check for DEFLECTION is not necessary.
<br>(i) Design and reinforcement detailing of one way slab (simply supported) and Concept of design of balcony slab.
<br>(ii) One way continuous slab (Two span only) using moment co-efficient as per IS: 456 Table 12.
<br>(iii) Design and reinforcement detailing of Two-way slab :
<br>a) Corners are not held down
<br>b) Corners are held down : All the Four edges discontinuous case only.
<br><br><b>[12 Hrs] [30 Marks] </b>

UNIT IV: Design of Column and Footings
<b>Design of Columns</b>: Concept of long and short columns, Specifications for main and lateral reinforcement, interaction diagram in column design, Behaviour of RCC column under axial load.
<br>(i) Design and detailing of Axially loaded short columns (circular, square and rectangular as per IS specifications), 
<br>(ii) Design of column subjected to uniaxial bending for reinforcement distributed equally on TWO sides only using SP-16 chart (Square and Rectangular) and concept of bi-axial bending.
<br><br><b>Design of Column Footings</b>: Concept of column footing, Design criteria, Design of square, rectangular isolated column footings, Detailing of reinforcement.
<br><br><b>[10 Hrs] [30 Marks] </b>

UNIT V: Design of Dog-legged Staircase
<b>Design of Staircase</b>: Introduction to stair cases, design and detailing of dog-legged stair, Single flight stairs.
<br><b>Lintel</b>: Design and Detailing of a Lintel
<br><br><b>[06 Hrs] [15 Marks] </b>

UNIT VI: Pre-Stressed Concrete
Concept of prestressing, Difference between RCC & PSC, Situations where prestressed concrete is used, Materials used in prestressed concrete and their specifications as per IS.  Pre-tensioning and Post-tensioning, Mention the systems of prestresses, Mention the Losses in Prestresses. (Excluding numerical problems)
<br><br><b>[04 Hrs] [15 Marks] </b>
