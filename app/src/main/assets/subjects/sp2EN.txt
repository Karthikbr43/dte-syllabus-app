<b>Experiments:</b>

<br><br><b>I: Theodolite</b>
<br>1.	Study of parts of a Transit Theodolite and its temporary adjustments
<br>2.	Measurement of horizontal angle by Repetition method
<br>3.	Measurement of horizontal angle by Reiteration method
<br>4.	Measurement of vertical angle
<br>5.	Open traversing using theodolite and plotting
<br>6.	Conducting a Closed traverse of a given area and balancing it by Bowditch rule and Transit rule

<br><br><b>II: Trigonometric Levelling</b>
<br>7.	Determination of height of an object whose base is accessible
<br>8.	Determination of height of an object whose base is inaccessible (single plane method instrument axes at different levels only-two cases)

<br><br><b>III: Tachometric Surveying</b>
<br>9.	Determination of Stadia constants
<br>10.	Determine horizontal distance by Horizontal sight
<br>11. Determine Horizontal distance and elevation for inclined sight with staff held vertical by Stadia hair method

<br><br><b>IV: Curves</b>
<br>12.	Setting out simple curve by Offsets from Long chord method
<br>13.	Setting out simple curve by Rankines method using Theodolite and Total station
<br>14.	Setting out Compound curves given two Radii by Deflection angle method

<br><br><b>V: Total Station</b>
<br>15.	Total station-general commands used- instrument preparation and setting-reading distances and angles
<br>16.	Measurement of distances and coordinates of given points, using EDM and Total station
<br>17.	Measurement of altitudes of given elevated points, using total station
<br>18.	Run a closed traverse using Total station and plotting the traverse
<br>19. Determination of areas of field (enclosed three or more points) by total station

<br><br><b>VI: Global Positioning System</b>
<br>20. Study of hand held GPS
<br>21.	Measurement of latitude, longitude and altitude using hand held GPS
<br>22. Selection and marking of routes using hand held GPS
