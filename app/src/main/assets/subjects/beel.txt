Unit – I: Tutorial and Graded Exercises

1.	Identification of passive components: 
<br>(i)	Given code/color bands/symbols, find the value, and 
<br>(ii) Given the value, find the code/pattern or bands of colors.
<br>(iii) Compare the values of passive components using color code/symbols and meter method.
<br><br>2.	Familiarization of Lab tools, tag-board, bread board, printed circuit board (PCB) and transformers.
<br><br>3.	Process and classification of soldering. List the precautions to be taken during soldering. Demonstration of qualitative soldering for simple circuits.
<br><br>4.	Sketch the front panels of laboratory equipment such as RPS, CRO, Multimeter, LCR meter and Signal Generator. List their functions. Familiarize with their operation and usage.
<br><br>5.	Demonstrate the use of Multimeters-Analog and Digital and discuss the precautionary measures to be taken while using them. Measuring of AC/DC voltages and currents using multimeters
<br><br>6.	Demonstrate the verification of Ohm’s law.
<br><br>7.	Experimental verification of effective resistance in the following cases using Ohm’s law.i. Series resistance network, ii. Parallel resistance network and iii. Combination network.
<br><br>8.	Measure effective inductance of inductors connected in i. Series, ii. Parallel and   iii. Series-Parallel combination using LCR meter and compare with the theoretical values.
<br><br>9.	Measure effective capacitance of capacitors connected in i. Series, ii. Parallel and   iii. Series-Parallel combination using LCR meter and compare with the theoretical values.
<br><br>10.	Demonstrate and observe the effect of cells connected in series and parallel.
<br><br>11.	Demonstrate Kirchhoff’s current law for a simple DC circuit.
<br><br>12.	Demonstrate Kirchhoff’s voltage law for a simple DC circuit.
<br><br>13.	Compute the impedance (magnitude) of a series RLC circuit using Ohm’s law and compare it with the theoretical value.
<br><br>14.	Demonstrate the operation of CRO: Measure AC and DC Voltages, Frequency and Phase angle.
<br><br>15.	Demonstrate the control of an electric lamp using a typical relay and measure the operating/release voltages.
<br><br>16.	Determine the characteristics of light dependent resistor (LDR).
<br><br>17.	Determine the characteristics of voltage dependent resistor (VDR).
<br><br>18.	Open-ended experiment of similar nature and magnitude of the above

Unit – II: Project Activities [CIE- 05 Marks]

1. Open-ended activity like
<br>(i) Collection of catalogues and specification sheets, preparation of a chart displaying symbols of passive components and connectors/cables. 
<br>(ii) Collection of the contributors (scientists) and contribution details to the field of Electrical and Electronics engineering (At least 10). Or
<br>(iii) Any other such activities that can contribute to the student’s knowledge in respect of this course.

<br><br>2. Record the best practices used in the disposal of E-waste and precautions in the operation of electrical appliances.

<br><br><b>Execution Mode</b>
<br><br>1.	Maximum of 4 students in each batch for project activity.
<br>2.	Project activity 1 and 2 are mandatory for every batch. 
<br>3.	Project activities shall be carried out throughout the semester and present the project report at the end of the semester. 
<br>4.	Report-size shall be qualitative and not to exceed 10 pages; one report per batch.
<br>5.	Each of the activity can be carried out off-class; however, demonstration/presentation should be done during laboratory sessions. 
<br>6.	Assessment shall be made based on quality of activity, presentation/demonstration and report.
